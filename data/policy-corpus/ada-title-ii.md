# Americans with Disabilities Act — Title II
## Relevance to Higher Education Accommodations

**Source:** U.S. Department of Justice, Americans with Disabilities Act  
**URL:** https://www.ada.gov/topics/education/  
**Accessed:** 2026-06-01

---

## What Title II Requires

Title II of the Americans with Disabilities Act prohibits discrimination against people with disabilities by public entities, including public colleges and universities. Public institutions must provide equal access to their programs, services, and activities.

This means a public college or university cannot deny a qualified student with a disability the opportunity to participate in or benefit from the institution's programs simply because of their disability.

---

## Who Is Protected

A student is protected under Title II if they have a physical or mental impairment that substantially limits one or more major life activities. Major life activities include learning, reading, concentrating, thinking, communicating, and working.

A student does not need to have a severe or permanent condition. A condition that substantially limits academic functioning qualifies.

---

## What Institutions Must Do

Public institutions must:

- Provide reasonable modifications to policies, practices, and procedures when necessary to avoid discrimination
- Ensure that communications with students with disabilities are as effective as communications with others
- Make reasonable accommodations that allow qualified students to access academic programs
- Engage in an interactive process with the student to identify appropriate accommodations

Institutions are not required to provide accommodations that would fundamentally alter the nature of a program or create an undue burden.

---

## Reasonable Accommodations in Academic Settings

Reasonable accommodations in higher education commonly include:

- Extended time on examinations
- Reduced distraction testing environments
- Permission to record lectures
- Alternative format course materials
- Note-taking assistance
- Flexible attendance policies where attendance is not a core learning outcome
- Written copies of verbal instructions
- Preferential seating

---

## The Interactive Process

When a student requests an accommodation, the institution must engage in an interactive process. This means the institution and student work together to identify what the student needs and what the institution can provide. The institution cannot simply deny a request without exploring alternatives.

---

## Self-Identification Is Required

Institutions are not required to identify students with disabilities on their own. Students must notify the institution of their disability and request accommodations. However, once notified, the institution has an obligation to respond promptly and in good faith.

---

## Documentation

Institutions may require documentation of a disability to verify eligibility. However, documentation requirements must be reasonable. Institutions cannot require exhaustive or expensive evaluations when simpler documentation would suffice. The purpose of documentation is to establish that the student has a disability and to help identify appropriate accommodations — not to create a barrier to access.

---

## Key Principle

The goal of Title II is not to give students with disabilities an advantage. It is to remove barriers that would otherwise prevent them from demonstrating their knowledge and abilities on an equal basis with their peers.
