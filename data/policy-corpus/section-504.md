# Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973
## Relevance to Higher Education Accommodations

**Source:** U.S. Department of Education, Office for Civil Rights  
**URL:** https://www2.ed.gov/about/offices/list/ocr/504faq.html  
**Accessed:** 2026-06-01

---

## What Section 504 Requires

Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 prohibits discrimination against people with disabilities by any program or activity receiving federal financial assistance. This includes virtually all colleges and universities in the United States, both public and private, because they receive federal funding.

Section 504 requires institutions to provide students with disabilities an equal opportunity to participate in and benefit from all programs, services, and activities.

---

## Who Is Protected

A student is protected under Section 504 if they have a physical or mental impairment that substantially limits one or more major life activities, have a record of such an impairment, or are regarded as having such an impairment.

Major life activities include learning, reading, concentrating, thinking, communicating, caring for oneself, and performing manual tasks.

---

## Difference From the ADA

Section 504 and the ADA overlap significantly in higher education. The key difference is scope. The ADA applies to public institutions under Title II and to private institutions under Title III. Section 504 applies to any institution receiving federal funding regardless of whether it is public or private.

In practice, most higher education institutions are covered by both laws simultaneously.

---

## What Institutions Must Provide

Under Section 504, institutions must:

- Make programs and activities accessible to students with disabilities
- Provide academic adjustments — modifications to academic requirements that are necessary to ensure they do not discriminate on the basis of disability
- Provide auxiliary aids and services when necessary to ensure effective communication
- Not impose rules that have the effect of limiting the participation of students with disabilities

---

## Academic Adjustments

Academic adjustments under Section 504 may include:

- Extended time for completing exams
- Substitution of specific courses required for the completion of degree requirements
- Adaptation of the manner in which specific courses are conducted
- Changes to the format of examinations or other evaluation methods

Institutions are not required to make adjustments that would fundamentally alter the essential nature of a program.

---

## Auxiliary Aids and Services

Auxiliary aids and services help ensure effective communication. These include:

- Taped texts and audio recordings
- Note-taking assistance
- Interpreters
- Text-to-speech and speech-to-text software
- Extended time
- Accessible formats for printed materials

---

## Documentation Under Section 504

Institutions may ask for documentation to support an accommodation request. However, the Office for Civil Rights has stated that documentation requirements must be reasonable and must not be so burdensome as to effectively deny access to accommodations.

Where a student's disability is not obvious, institutions may request documentation from a qualified professional. However, they may not require documentation that is unduly expensive or difficult to obtain.

---

## The Student's Responsibility

Under Section 504, students are responsible for identifying themselves to the institution as having a disability and requesting accommodations. Unlike in elementary and secondary school, colleges and universities are not required to identify students with disabilities and provide services without a request.

However, once a student makes a request, the institution must respond in a timely manner and must engage in good faith to identify appropriate accommodations.

---

## Grievance Procedures

Institutions receiving federal funding must have grievance procedures that provide for the prompt and equitable resolution of complaints alleging discrimination on the basis of disability. Students who believe their rights have been violated can file a complaint with the institution's disability services office or directly with the U.S. Department of Education's Office for Civil Rights.

---

## Key Principle

Section 504 exists because Congress recognized that exclusion of people with disabilities from federally funded programs is a form of discrimination. In higher education, this means that a student's disability should never be the reason they are unable to access the education they have earned the right to receive.
