# Higher Education Disability Services — General Best Practices
## Institutional Policies and Student Rights

**Source:** Association on Higher Education and Disability (AHEAD) Best Practice Guidelines  
**URL:** https://www.ahead.org/professional-resources/best-practices  
**Accessed:** 2026-06-01

---

## The Role of Disability Services in Higher Education

Disability services offices in higher education exist to ensure that students with disabilities have equal access to academic programs. Their role is not to lower academic standards — it is to remove barriers that would otherwise prevent a student from demonstrating their abilities.

Disability services coordinators are trained professionals whose job is to work with students, not against them. An intake meeting with a disability services coordinator is a collaborative conversation, not an evaluation or a test.

---

## How Accommodation Requests Work

The standard process for obtaining accommodations at most higher education institutions follows these general steps:

1. The student contacts the disability services office and registers as a student with a disability
2. The student provides documentation supporting their accommodation request
3. The student meets with a disability services coordinator for an intake meeting
4. The coordinator determines appropriate accommodations based on the student's needs and documentation
5. The student receives an official accommodation letter
6. The student shares the accommodation letter with relevant professors each semester

This process varies by institution. Students should always check their specific institution's disability services website for the exact process.

---

## Documentation Guidelines

Most institutions follow documentation guidelines developed by AHEAD or similar professional organizations. Under these guidelines:

- Documentation should come from a qualified professional with expertise in the relevant disability
- Documentation should describe the student's functional limitations in academic settings
- Documentation should be reasonably current — typically within three to five years for most conditions
- Documentation requirements should be proportionate to the accommodation being requested

Where formal documentation is unavailable or unaffordable, many institutions have alternative pathways including self-disclosure processes, provisional accommodations, or referrals to low-cost evaluation services.

---

## Confidentiality

Disability-related information is confidential. Disability services offices are required to keep student disability information separate from general academic records. Professors are notified of a student's accommodations through an accommodation letter but are not told the nature of the student's disability unless the student chooses to disclose it.

---

## Provisional Accommodations

Many institutions offer provisional accommodations for students who are in the process of obtaining documentation or who have a documented disability but have not yet completed the full registration process. Students experiencing academic difficulty due to a disability should contact disability services immediately rather than waiting until documentation is complete.

---

## Renewals and Ongoing Accommodations

At most institutions, accommodation letters must be renewed each semester. The student is responsible for requesting renewal and for distributing their updated accommodation letter to professors. Some institutions offer multi-semester or annual letters — students should ask their disability services coordinator about their institution's renewal policy.

---

## When a Professor Disputes an Accommodation

If a professor refuses to honor an accommodation letter or disputes the appropriateness of a specific accommodation, the student should:

1. Contact the disability services office immediately and explain the situation
2. Ask the disability services coordinator to communicate directly with the professor
3. If the issue is not resolved, request a formal review through the disability services office

Students are not required to negotiate directly with professors about the validity of their accommodations. That is the disability services office's role.

---

## Requesting New Accommodations

If a student's needs change or they identify a new barrier that their current accommodations do not address, they can return to disability services at any time to request additional accommodations. There is no limit on how many times a student can update their accommodation plan.

---

## Students Without a Formal Diagnosis

Students who suspect they have a disability but have not been formally diagnosed can still contact disability services. Many coordinators can provide guidance on how to seek an evaluation, connect students with campus or community resources for low-cost assessments, and discuss what options may be available in the interim.

A formal diagnosis is the standard pathway to accommodations, but it is not always the only pathway. Students should have this conversation directly with their institution's disability services office.

---

## Key Principle

Accommodations are not special treatment. They are a professional and legal recognition that the standard academic environment was designed for a specific type of learner, and that students whose brains work differently deserve access to the same opportunities. Requesting accommodations is an act of self-advocacy, not weakness.
